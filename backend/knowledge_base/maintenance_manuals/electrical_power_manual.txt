ELECTRICAL POWER SYSTEM — MAINTENANCE MANUAL
============================================

Section 1: Normal Operating Parameters
---------------------------------------
Normal power consumption range: 20% - 80% of rated capacity
Critical overload threshold: 95% of rated capacity
Power outage detection: consumption drops to 0 kW
Monitoring frequency: every 2 minutes

Section 2: Power Outage — Root Causes
--------------------------------------
1. MAIN CIRCUIT BREAKER TRIP
   - Symptoms: Complete power loss to tower or floor
   - Cause: Overload, short circuit, ground fault
   - Action: Identify tripped breaker, reset after fault cleared, inspect wiring
   - Estimated repair time: 30 minutes - 2 hours

2. TRANSFORMER FAILURE
   - Symptoms: Power loss to multiple towers simultaneously
   - Cause: Overheating, insulation breakdown, lightning strike
   - Action: Contact electricity board, switch to backup DG set
   - Estimated repair time: 4-24 hours (utility dependent)

3. DG SET FAILURE
   - Symptoms: No backup power during outage
   - Cause: Fuel shortage, battery failure, alternator fault
   - Action: Check fuel level, inspect battery, start manual override
   - Estimated repair time: 1-4 hours

4. SUBSTATION FAULT
   - Cause: Equipment age, moisture ingress, rodent damage
   - Action: Isolate substation, call certified HV electrician
   - Estimated repair time: 6-12 hours

Section 3: Power Overload — Root Causes
----------------------------------------
1. EXCESSIVE APPLIANCE LOAD
   - Cause: Multiple high-power appliances running simultaneously
   - Action: Load shedding, notify residents to reduce usage, check load distribution

2. FAULTY WIRING
   - Cause: Damaged insulation, loose connections, undersized cables
   - Action: Infrared thermal inspection, rewire affected sections

3. POWER FACTOR ISSUES
   - Cause: Inductive loads (motors, ACs), poor power factor correction
   - Action: Install/service power factor correction capacitors

Section 4: Emergency Procedures
---------------------------------
1. Activate backup DG set within 60 seconds of outage
2. Ensure lifts are safely parked at ground floor
3. Activate emergency lighting in corridors and staircases
4. Notify residents of estimated restoration time
5. Contact electricity board for grid faults

Section 5: Contractor Requirements
------------------------------------
Power outages requiring licensed electricians (Grade A or B license)
Transformer work: HV-certified contractor only
DG maintenance: OEM-authorized service center preferred
