WATER PRESSURE SYSTEM — MAINTENANCE MANUAL
==========================================

Section 1: Normal Operating Parameters
---------------------------------------
Normal water pressure range: 2.0 - 6.0 bar
Critical low pressure threshold: 0.5 bar
Critical high pressure threshold: 8.0 bar
Pressure monitoring frequency: every 5 minutes

Section 2: Low Water Pressure — Root Causes
--------------------------------------------
1. PUMP FAILURE
   - Symptoms: sudden pressure drop below 1.0 bar
   - Cause: Motor burnout, impeller damage, electrical fault
   - Action: Inspect pump motor, check electrical connections, replace impeller if damaged
   - Estimated repair time: 4-8 hours

2. PIPE BURST OR LEAK
   - Symptoms: gradual pressure drop with wet patches or water logging
   - Cause: Corrosion, physical damage, freezing, high pressure surge
   - Action: Shut main valve, identify burst location, replace damaged section
   - Estimated repair time: 2-6 hours

3. VALVE MALFUNCTION
   - Symptoms: pressure drop in specific zone
   - Cause: Stuck valve, sediment buildup, worn gasket
   - Action: Inspect isolation valves, flush sediment, replace gaskets
   - Estimated repair time: 1-3 hours

4. FILTER BLOCKAGE
   - Symptoms: gradual pressure reduction over days/weeks
   - Cause: Sediment accumulation, mineral deposits
   - Action: Clean or replace pressure filters, flush lines
   - Estimated repair time: 1-2 hours

Section 3: High Water Pressure — Root Causes
---------------------------------------------
1. PRESSURE REGULATOR FAILURE
   - Cause: Worn diaphragm, spring failure, debris in regulator
   - Action: Replace pressure reducing valve (PRV), test with gauge
   - Critical: High pressure can burst pipes — act immediately

2. MUNICIPAL SUPPLY SURGE
   - Cause: External supply pressure spikes
   - Action: Install or repair pressure limiting valve at entry point

Section 4: Emergency Procedures
---------------------------------
1. Shut main water supply valve immediately
2. Notify all residents via PA system
3. Contact on-call plumber
4. Open emergency water tanks (if available)
5. Document incident with timestamp and sensor readings

Section 5: Preventive Maintenance Schedule
-------------------------------------------
Monthly: Visual inspection of pumps and valves
Quarterly: Pressure gauge calibration
Semi-annual: Filter cleaning/replacement
Annual: Full pipeline inspection, pump service
